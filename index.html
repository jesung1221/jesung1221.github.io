JaeSeong Kim

Hi! I'm JaeSeong Kim, a recent M.Sc. graduate in Electrical & Electronics Engineering from Tel Aviv University in Israel, where I also completed my B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering. I specialized in computer vision and computational imaging, with research focused on polarization-based face anti-spoofing and computational photography for consumer cameras.

During my Master's, I worked with Prof. David Mendlovic on novel polarization camera designs, resulting in a first-author IEEE Sensors Journal paper and a related patent (WO 2024/161329). Previously, I was an Image Quality Engineer at Samsung Corephotonics, where I worked on ISP tuning, assessing image quality factors, and camera-sensor performance evaluation.

Broadly, I am interested in computational photography, camera-sensor innovation, and imaging algorithms that enhance everyday devices. I am currently preparing for PhD applications in these fields.

JaeSeong Kim
The Crime Analysis Model is an intelligent surveillance system that classifies ten types of crime from video footage using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). The model employs a two-stage architecture: an anomaly detector first identifies time frames with unusual activity, then a crime classifier analyzes skeletal movement patterns to categorize incidents into one of ten categories: Abuse, Arson, Assault, Burglary, Fighting, Robbery, Shooting, Shoplifting, Stealing, or Vandalism. This approach optimizes efficiency by focusing classification resources only on segments containing potential criminal activity.
